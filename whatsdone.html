This table is neither exhaustive nor representative of the current state of things. These are merely some current examples that I have chose to highlight.

Country State of plastic ban?
China Single-use and non-degradable plastic bags will be banned in major Chinese cities by the end of 2020 and across the country by 2022.
US There is no national plastic bag fee or ban currently in effect in the United States. However, the states of California, Hawaii (de facto), New York, and Oregon, and the territories of American Samoa, Guam, Northern Mariana Islands, US Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico have banned disposable bags.

Companies' sustainability efforts

This table is neither exhaustive nor representative of the current state of things. These are merely some current examples that I have chose to highlight.

Source: The Guardian

Industry Sustainability efforts
Food & Beverages Food and drink firms including Unilever, Mars, Danone, Pepsico and Coca-Cola pledged to reduce the amount of virgin plastic they used by 2025 and to increase the amount of recycled plastic. The global commitment on plastic, introduced in late 2018 to get corporations to pledge to use less and recycle more, grew to more than 400 of the world’s biggest companies. Together they are responsible for more than 20% of all the plastic packaging produced.
Supermarket The UK supermarkets Tesco, Sainsbury’s, Asda, Morrisons and Waitrose raced to ditch hard-to-recycle “black” plastic from their ranges and to accelerate the amount of recycled material they use. Asda stated that nearly one-third of its plastic packaging would come from recycled sources by the end of 2020 and all should be recyclable by 2025. Waitrose said it had removed 90% of the 2,291 tonnes of black plastic. Some initiatives were eye-catching: Tesco pledged to remove 1bn pieces of plastic from products for sale in UK stores by the end of 2020.
